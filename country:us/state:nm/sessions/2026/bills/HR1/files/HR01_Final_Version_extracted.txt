1 A RESOLUTION
2 CREATING AND EMPOWERING A SPECIAL COMMITTEE OF THE HOUSE OF
3 REPRESENTATIVES TO INVESTIGATE ALLEGATIONS OF CRIMINAL
4 ACTIVITY AND PUBLIC CORRUPTION FOR THE PURPOSE OF SECURING
5 INFORMATION TO DETERMINE WHETHER LEGISLATION OR OTHER
6 LEGISLATIVE ACTION IS NECESSARY TO PROVIDE FOR SAFETY AND
7 WELFARE OR TO PROVIDE FOR PUBLIC CONFIDENCE IN STATE
8 GOVERNMENT.
9
10 WHEREAS, "Zorro ranch" was the name of a private estate
11 located in Santa Fe county consisting of approximately ten
12 thousand acres of real property, which included public land
13 leaseholds; and
14 WHEREAS, Zorro ranch and the persons who owned Zorro
15 ranch between 1993 and 2023 have been the subjects of great
16 public concern since at least 2019; however, previous
17 legislatures have not responded to this great public concern;
18 and
19 WHEREAS, members of the house of representatives
20 reported to the interim courts, corrections and justice
21 committee about allegations the members received that Zorro
22 ranch was an instrumentality of serious criminal activity
23 that was not investigated, prosecuted or otherwise acted upon
24 by state or local officers before potential investigations,
25 prosecutions or other actions were time barred pursuant to HR 1
Page 1

1 state law; and
2 WHEREAS, the house of representatives is concerned that
3 the failure to investigate the alleged criminal activity at
4 Zorro ranch and the risk of potential consequences of that
5 activity continue to affect the safety and welfare of the
6 state and that continued legislative inaction threatens
7 public confidence in state government; and
8 WHEREAS, Article 4, Section 2 of the constitution of New
9 Mexico vests the legislature with all powers necessary to the
10 legislature of a free state, which include the indispensable
11 power of inquiry, through which either chamber of the
12 legislature may conduct investigative procedures to secure
13 information necessary to legislate, to provide general
14 oversight of the administration of government, the
15 expenditure of public money and the use of public resources
16 and to exercise particular enumerated powers and duties; and
17 WHEREAS, as an example of the particular enumerated
18 powers and duties of the legislature, Article 4, Section 35
19 of the constitution of New Mexico vests the house of
20 representatives with the sole power of impeachment, which
21 confers a unique duty to oversee the conduct of state
22 officers and compels the investigation of allegations of
23 misconduct; and
24 WHEREAS, with each chamber of the legislature having the
25 power to determine the rules for the chamber's own procedures HR 1
Page 2

1 in accordance with Article 4, Section 11 of the constitution
2 of New Mexico, it is for the house of representatives to
3 decide whether to exercise the power of inquiry, the time and
4 place at which the power will be exercised and the manner in
5 which the power will be exercised;
6 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF
7 REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO that the speaker
8 appoint a special committee of the house of representatives
9 consisting of two members from the majority party and two
10 members from the minority party and designate a chair from
11 among the appointed members, and that the committee
12 investigate Zorro ranch and the related allegations of
13 criminal activity to determine whether legislation or other
14 legislative action is needed to:
15 A. prevent and properly punish crime;
16 B. protect children and other vulnerable persons;
17 C. establish sufficient procedures for securing
18 civil justice;
19 D. ensure proper use and stewardship of public
20 money and resources; and
21 E. provide for public accountability; and
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the committee be vested with
23 all necessary powers and process, including the powers to
24 conduct hearings, compel the attendance of witnesses,
25 administer oaths and issue subpoenas, and that the committee HR 1
Page 3

1 be authorized to function after the legislature adjourns sine
2 die; provided that the committee shall not function after
3 December 31, 2026; and provided further that process for the
4 committee shall be issued by the chair and, while the
5 legislature is in session, such process shall be served by
6 the sergeant-at-arms and enforced by the house of
7 representatives and, after the legislature adjourns, such
8 process shall be enforced in any court with jurisdiction over
9 the process and shall be in compliance with the applicable
10 rules of the court; and
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the chief clerk of the house
12 of representatives provide staff for the committee while the
13 legislature is in session and that the legislative council
14 service be requested to provide staff for the committee after
15 the legislature adjourns but, subject to the availability of
16 funding, the committee may retain additional staff, including
17 legal counsel, during the time the committee is properly
18 functioning; and
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the committee keep records
20 of its work, furnish an interim report to the leadership of
21 the house of representatives on or before July 31, 2026 and
22 furnish a final report to all members of the house of
23 representatives on or before December 31, 2026 that includes
24 a description of the investigative procedures carried out by
25 the committee, the findings and conclusions the committee HR 1
Page 4

1 made through carrying out those procedures and the
2 committee's recommendations; and
3 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this resolution be
4 transmitted to the chief clerk of the house of
5 representatives, the president pro tempore of the senate, the
6 New Mexico legislative council and the director of the
7 legislative council service. HR 1
